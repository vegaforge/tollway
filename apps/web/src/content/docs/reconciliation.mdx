---
title: Reconciliation
description: Matching receipts, commitments, and on-chain settlement
---

Reconciliation is the safety net that checks Tollway's off-chain records against
on-chain settlement. It is most important for channel mode because most channel
payments are cumulative off-chain commitments until close.

Read the source design section: [Reconciliation](https://github.com/vegaforge/tollway/blob/main/docs/design.md#reconciliation).

## Channel reconciliation

For MPP channel mode, Tollway compares the stored commitment stream with the
channel contract state and final close transaction. It should detect drift such
as a missing commitment, an unexpected remainder, or an under-settlement.

## Per-request reconciliation

For x402 and MPP charge, reconciliation is simpler: each receipt should map to a
settled transaction, and that transaction should match the receipt amount,
asset, counterparty, and resource reference.

## Response to drift

When reconciliation finds drift, Tollway should flag the event, stop additional
commitments for the counterparty when appropriate, and make the evidence
available to operators through exports or webhooks.
